Treatment Options

Six modalities,
one practitioner.

Each modality is selected based on your intake, tissue response, and treatment goal — not a menu you order from. The techniques work together.

01
Injury Rehabilitation

Targeted soft-tissue work following acute injury, post-surgical recovery, or motor vehicle accidents. Structured to reduce inflammation, restore range, and rebuild functional movement.

Clinical Focus
02
Myofascial Trigger Point Therapy

Direct compression and release of hyperirritable nodules within taut bands of muscle. Addresses referral pain patterns, local tenderness, and the neurological loops that keep muscles locked.

Neuromuscular
03
General Swedish Massage

The foundational technique. Effleurage, petrissage, tapotement, and friction applied systemically to improve circulation, reduce cortisol, and ease diffuse muscular tension.

Relaxation · Recovery
04
Sports Massage

Pre-event preparation, inter-competition maintenance, and post-event recovery protocols designed for athletes at every level. Work focuses on sport-specific loading patterns, repetitive use areas, and performance retention — without the guesswork of generic wellness massage.

Performance · Recovery
05
Myofascial Cupping

Negative-pressure silicone cupping applied along fascial lines to decompress tissue, increase local circulation, and create a gliding release that compression-based work cannot replicate.

Fascial · Decompression
06
Myofascial Release

Sustained, low-load pressure applied to the fascial system to release restrictions, reduce pain, and restore three-dimensional movement. Complements all other modalities.

Clinical Method

Reasoning before
technique.

Good massage is not about pressure or duration. It is about reading the tissue, understanding the history, and choosing the right intervention — then adjusting in real time as the body responds. This is what separates a clinical RMT from a spa treatment.

  • Intake assessment and orthopedic screening before every session to identify tissue priorities and contraindications.
  • Treatment planning built around your specific presentation — not a fixed-duration menu item.
  • Intra-session adjustments as the tissue responds. Depth, rhythm, and technique shift throughout.
  • Post-treatment guidance: movement recommendations, hydration, and home-care strategies that extend the work.
